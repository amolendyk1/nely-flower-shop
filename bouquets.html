Bouquets

Beautifully curated bouquets for every occasion. Each design includes care instructions and the story behind the pairings.

Romantic Bloom

A classic expression of love

Flowers included: Red roses, lavender, baby's breath, eucalyptus

Care: Trim stems at an angle every 2 days. Change water daily. Keep away from direct sunlight and drafts.

Why they pair well: The rich elegance of roses is softened by lavender's calming purple, while baby's breath adds airy volume and eucalyptus brings a fresh aromatic finish.

Sunshine Garden

Bright, cheerful, and full of life

Flowers included: Sunflowers, yellow tulips, white daisies, lavender

Care: Keep in a cool spot. Mist petals lightly. Remove any wilting blooms promptly to extend vase life.

Why they pair well: Sunflowers and tulips create a bold, happy silhouette, while daisies add delicate charm and lavender introduces a soothing fragrance and color contrast.

Elegant Whisper

Soft, sophisticated, and timeless

Flowers included: Peonies, hydrangeas, irises, baby's breath

Care: Hydrangeas need frequent misting. Peonies benefit from support. Keep in a cool room away from fruit (ethylene shortens bloom life).

Why they pair well: The lush fullness of peonies and hydrangeas creates a dreamy base, while irises add vertical elegance and baby's breath ties it all together with airy lightness.

Meadow Fresh

Wild and carefree

Flowers included: Daisies, lavender, baby's breath, eucalyptus, tulips

Care: Trim stems every 2 days. Keep water fresh. Display in a bright spot but out of direct afternoon sun.

Why they pair well: Daisies and tulips bring a field-fresh feel, lavender adds fragrance and color depth, and eucalyptus with baby's breath create natural texture and contrast.

Royal Velvet

Rich, bold, and dramatic

Flowers included: Purple roses, irises, lavender, eucalyptus

Care: Roses benefit from warm water initially. Remove thorns carefully. Keep irises separate until they open fully.

Why they pair well: The deep purple tones of roses and irises create a regal palette, while lavender adds soft texture and eucalyptus contributes a crisp green contrast.

Spring Dream

Fresh blooms for a new season

Flowers included: Tulips, daffodils, hyacinths, iris, baby's breath

Care: Tulips continue to grow in the vase — trim every 2 days. Daffodils secrete a sap that harms other flowers; condition separately for 24 hours first.

Why they pair well: Tulips and daffodils are the heralds of spring, hyacinths bring intoxicating fragrance, and irises add striking form while baby's breath softens the arrangement.
